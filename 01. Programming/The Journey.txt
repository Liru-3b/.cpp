
D/M/Y: 4/4/2026
   1. std::string can be used to print repetitive symbols as opposed to using a loop.

   2. A Task List gives you ultimate freedom, at the cost of memory and speed.
      Best for dynamic plugins, undo/redo systems, or multi-threaded schedulers.

   3. Merging literals ("A" "B") is faster than multiple cout calls, 
      though negligible in practice. For real performance, avoid std::endl:
      It forces a slow buffer flush. Use '\n' instead.

   4. Early Returns reduce cognitive load for you and the compiler.
      Though exiting once you have your answer might not be the best option.
      When to avoid? When you need "Atomic Transactions" 
      (where multiple steps must succeed or fail together) 
      or data aggregation or filtering (building a result over time).

   5. std::cin is "greedy"—it grabs numbers and leaves "trash"  
      in the input buffer to break your next read.
      Fixed using getline to grab the whole line and std::stod to parse it.
